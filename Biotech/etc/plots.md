Ok. Sometimes it is fun to make your own type of plots which nobody else had done before for the given parameters,

- e.g. Yang et al 2020: we made pie charts of the number and distribution of sizes of neurospheres with the size of each pie corresponding to the total number.
- Or Vinnikov et al JNeurosci 2014: overlap of 5 prediction tools in a form of Euler diagram where one of the ovals was modified so that the sizes of each figure corresponded to the overall number of hits.
