The term "vitamin D" refers to two chemical compounds - ergosterol (vitamin D2) and cholecalciferol (vitamin D3).

Active vitamin D3 is correctly regarded as a neurohormone due to its pleiotropic biological activities.

Relation between vitamin D and calcium-phosphorus metabolism

other roles vitamin D:

- vitamin D immunomodulation includes attenuation and stimulation of Th1 and Th2 cell proliferation

- vitamin D can induce or inhibit the synthesis, secretion, and release of anti- inflammatory (IL-4 and IL-10) and pro-inflammatory (IL-1, TNF-α, IFN-γ) cytokines

The optimal serum concentration of vitamin D has been considered to lead not to a PTH elevation.

Calcium and vitamin D supplementation have been shown to reduce secondary hyperparathyroidism and play a role in age-related osteoporosis.
