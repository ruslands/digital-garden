Sumak (Turkish: Sumak) refers to sumac, a type of edible plant widely used in Middle Eastern, Mediterranean, and Turkish cuisines.

- Botanical Name: Rhus coriaria
- Description: Sumac is a flowering plant that produces deep red berries. These berries are dried and ground into a coarse, reddish-purple powder.
- Flavor Profile: It has a tangy, lemony flavor with a hint of fruitiness, making it a popular spice for adding a citrusy kick to dishes.
- Uses in Turkish Cuisine:

- Sprinkled over salads like çoban salatası (shepherd’s salad).
- Used as a garnish for grilled meats, kebabs, and fish.
- Mixed into yogurt or dips like cacık or hummus.
- Added to rice and pilaf for extra flavor.

Sumac is not only flavorful but also rich in antioxidants and believed to have various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory properties.
