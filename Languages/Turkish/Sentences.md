- Nasılsın? (How are you?) This is a common greeting in Turkish. The response can be "iyiyim" (I'm good) or "teşekkür ederim, sen nasılsın?" (Thank you, and how are you?)

- Adın ne? (What's your name?) This is a way to ask someone's name. The response can be "Benim adım Ahmet" (My name is Ahmet).

- Nerede yaşıyorsun? (Where do you live?) This is a way to ask someone where they live. The response can be "İstanbul'da yaşıyorum" (I live in Istanbul).

- Hangi dili konuşuyorsun? (Which language do you speak?) This is a way to ask someone which language they speak. The response can be "Türkçe konuşuyorum" (I speak Turkish) or "İngilizce konuşuyorum" (I speak English).

- Ne yapıyorsun? (What are you doing?) This is a way to ask someone what they are doing. The response can be "Çalışıyorum" (I'm working) or "Dinleniyorum" (I'm resting).

- Bir su isteyebilir miyim? (Can I have a water, please?) This is a way to ask for something politely. The response can be "Tabii, buyurun" (Sure, here you go).

- İstanbul'da nereye gitmek istersin? (Where would you like to go in Istanbul?) This is a way to ask someone where they would like to go. The response can be "Ayasofya'ya gitmek istiyorum" (I want to go to the Hagia Sophia).

- Ne zaman geleceksin? (When will you come?) This is a way to ask someone when they will come. The response can be "Yarın geleceğim" (I will come tomorrow).

- Ben Türkiye'ye gitmek istiyorum. (I want to go to Turkey.) In this sentence, "ben" means "I," "Türkiye'ye" means "to Turkey," and "gitmek" means "to go." "İstiyorum" means "I want." Notice how the verb "gitmek" comes after the object "Türkiye'ye." In Turkish, the verb typically comes at the end of the sentence.

- Bugün okula gitmedim çünkü hastaydım. (I didn't go to school today because I was sick.) In this sentence, "bugün" means "today," "okula" means "to school," and "gitmedim" means "I didn't go." "Çünkü" means "because," and "hastaydım" means "I was sick." Notice how the conjunction "çünkü" connects the two clauses, and the verb "gitmedim" comes after the subject "I."

- Kitaplarınızı okuduk ve beğendik. (We read your books and liked them.) In this sentence, "kitaplarınızı" means "your books," "okuduk" means "we read," and "beğendik" means "we liked." Notice how the object "kitaplarınızı" comes before the verb "okuduk," but after the subject "we," and how the verb "beğendik" comes at the end of the sentence.

- Çok üzgünüm - I'm very sorry, I regret it. This phrase can be used to express condolences for any occasion.
- Elinize sağlık - May your hands be blessed. If you enjoyed a meal and want to express your gratitude to the host, say "Elinize sağlık!"
- Kolay gelsin - Good luck. This is a wish for good luck or a good work to anyone who is going to do something or is currently busy with work.
- Geçmiş olsun - Get well soon, may it pass quickly. This wish is appropriate for illness, if someone has recovered or has gone through unpleasant moments in life. After completing a journey, on buses or trains, passengers may hear this phrase - it is believed that even the most comfortable journey causes discomfort.
- Çok yaşa - Long live. If someone sneezes in Turkey, people say "Çok yaşa," and the response can be "Sen de gör" which means "I wish you to see it too."
- Sıhhatler olsun - With renewal of health. This is a special wish for health and is said to someone who has just showered or got a haircut. It's similar to saying "With ease!"
- Mutlu Bayram - happy bayram

Anlamiyorum / Anlam-iyor-um

- Root: anlamak (to understand)
- anlam: meaning
- -iyor: present continuous tense suffix
- -um: first person singular suffix

Anladim / Anladim - I understood

- Anla: The root of the word is "anla-", which comes from the verb "anlamak," meaning "to understand" in Turkish.
- -dı: The suffix "-dı" is added to the root "anla-" to indicate the past tense, which makes the word "anladı." This means "he/she/it understood" in Turkish.
- -m: The final suffix "-m" is added to "anladı" to indicate the first-person singular pronoun, which makes the word "anladım." This means "I understood" in Turkish.

Anliyorum / Anl-iyor-um  - I understand

- "Anlamak" is the infinitive form of the verb, which means "to understand."
- The suffix "-iyor" is added to the stem of the verb to form the present continuous tense, which means "I am understanding."
- The suffix "-um" is added to the end of the verb to indicate that the subject is first person singular, which means "I."

Konuşuyorsun / konuşuyorsun - you are speaking

- Konuş-: root, which means "to speak" in Turkish.
- -uyor-: present continuous tense suffix, which indicates that the action is ongoing in the present.
- -sun: personal suffix, which indicates the subject of the sentence. In this case, the suffix "-sun" is used for the second person singular "you" in Turkish.

Therefore, "konuşuyorsun" translates to "you are speaking" in English
